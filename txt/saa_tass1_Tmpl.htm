Last year, over 38 million retired American workers received Social Security benefits. Next year that number's set to go up. And the next year...and the next.

The U.S. Social Security program is the largest government program in the world and our single greatest expenditure. When it began in 1935 the world was a different place, Social Security was self-financing, and 17 American workers contributed to every retiree.

YOU'RE NOT GOING TO GET THE CHECK YOU EXPECTED
By 2035, that ratio will have fallen to 2.1 workers per retiree. At exactly the same time as 40 million of us so-called 'baby boomers' reach retirement age.

So if you're hoping for Social Security to cover the amount of retirement expenses it covers today -- think again. That's never going to happen.

Although we (workers) have to pay in, they (the government) can change the rules regarding our benefit eligibility at any time they like! Workers paying into the Social Security system are not guaranteed to receive Social Security benefits.

All because Social Security is an entitlement program and Congress has the power to change, end or deny payments.

And they have no intention of paying you anything like what you paid in!
 

There's anger everywhere about the spending of the Social Security Trust Fund and widespread despair by seniors approaching retirement that they will be unable to live on the amount the government will give them when the Trust Fund runs out.

Right now, the amount of money the federal government has "borrowed" from the so-called Social Security trust fund just crossed the $5 trillion mark. Politicians say it isn't really debt; that it's money the government "owes itself."

THE FEDERAL GOVERNMENT IS GOING TO STIFF YOU
I say that's nonsense. That $5 trillion is the property of us — America's future retirees. The only way this debt "doesn't count" is if the government has no intention of paying its obligations to America's retirees.

That $5 trillion is going to become a problem in 2020 when the system will start paying out more than it's taking in.

By then, the federal government will need to find more than half a trillion dollars each year to make good on its retirement promise to seniors.

The trouble is, finding an extra half-trillion dollars will mean either raising taxes by a third or doubling the current deficit.

Which D.C. won't do.

Instead, they'll look to squeeze the middle class and anyone who had a good job with a pension, or anyone who made their own provisions for old age.

MEANS TESTING WILL MEAN MILLIONS WON'T GET SOCIAL SECURITY  
They will introduce means tests for Social Security and "redistribute" wealth in the name of economic equality.

Which means you're very likely to be the one to lose your Social Security check!

Here at SAA, we're going to fight to maintain Social Security but we are also getting behind a movement to give seniors more choice.

Could privatization of Social Security be the answer?

Many free market economists have been calling for Social Security to be privatized. Privatization opponents believe privatization is dangerous because financial markets are by nature volatile and risky — while Social Security is safe because the Social Security trust fund invests only in U.S. Treasury Bills.

But because Congress can do what it likes
with Social Security -- is it really safe???

CONGRESS WELSHED ONCE BEFORE
Congress can change the rules of the game -- any time it pleases. It did so in the 1980s when it started taxing Social Security benefits, effectively cutting benefits for many retirees.
 
And it will change the rules again sometime around 2020 when payments out will exceed contributions received. In fact, Congress is a risk. A big risk. Private retirement accounts could actually be safer than Social Security!

This is how Social Security works -- or doesn't!
For every dollar you pay in to the federal government SSA, you can expect to get 1% back. That's a terrible rate of return and it's not enough to make any difference to your retirement. The average 40-year old is better off walking away from it and contributing to a private retirement account — giving up any future benefits in exchange for no longer paying the Social Security tax. 

HOW ABOUT WE LET PEOPLE FIX IT? 
Having paid in until age 40, these workers would support current retirees, but would never themselves become drains on an otherwise bankrupt system.

It sounds simple but it could work!

Economists have looked at the numbers and identified that the group remaining in the system would be the long-term poor. People who are never expected to earn enough to make leaving the existing system worthwhile. These are exactly the people who Social Security should be helping. It looks like privatization is a win-win for all Americans.

Allowing Americans a one-shot choice at opting out of Social Security at age 40 or above would both shrink Social Security to a financially prudent size and return its mission to that of a safety net for the poor.
